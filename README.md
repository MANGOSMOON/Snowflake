Snowflakes are ice crystals that form in clouds when water vapor freezes. Their structure is influenced by environmental conditions such as:

- **Temperature**
- **Humidity**
- **Air currents**

Each snowflake develops a hexagonal (six-sided) structure due to the molecular arrangement of water molecules when they freeze.
